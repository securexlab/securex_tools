The Bikram Sambat (BS) calendar is the official, historical calendar of Nepal. While much of the world operates on the Gregorian (AD) system, Nepal’s daily life, financial systems, and festivals are deeply tied to the lunar and solar cycles tracked by the BS calendar.

### How Bikram Sambat Works
Unlike the Gregorian calendar, which has fixed leap years and predictable month lengths, the Bikram Sambat calendar is solar-lunar. A month in the BS calendar is determined by the time it takes the sun to transition through a zodiac sign. Because the Earth's orbit is elliptical, this time varies. 

This means a Nepali month like Baishakh or Kartik can have anywhere from 29 to 32 days, and it changes every single year!
