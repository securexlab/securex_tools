Nepal is undergoing a massive digital transformation. From digital banking and e-governance to the rapid growth of local IT outsourcing companies, the nation is connected more than ever before. However, with digital growth comes the inevitable rise of cyber threats.

### The Shifting Threat Landscape
Historically, many local businesses relied on basic firewalls and consumer-grade antivirus software. Today, the threats are much more sophisticated:
* Ransomware targeting small-to-medium enterprises.
* Phishing attacks aimed at stealing employee credentials.
* Vulnerabilities in custom-built, legacy web applications.

### Moving from Reactive to Proactive
The old model of cybersecurity was reactive—waiting for a breach to happen and then trying to fix it. The new standard for IT companies must be proactive defense. 

This includes implementing robust threat telemetry, adaptive risk scoring, and deception technology. By setting up "honeypots" and tracking unauthorized internal movement, IT administrators can isolate attackers before they ever reach critical data.
